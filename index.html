Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, founded Grameen Bank, pioneering microfinance to alleviate poverty. His innovative approach provided small loans to the impoverished without collateral, empowering millions, especially women, to build sustainable livelihoods. Yunus's work revolutionized economic development and inspired global microcredit movements.
